Feedback

072015

So far, pairing with others has helped me in more ways than one.

I think what I enjoy most about pairing is the idea that we both have to work collaboratively to solve a problem. Not only do we get insight into what and how the other person is thinking, but we get to learn from them new ways to solve the same problem.

Receiving feedback has definitely helped me with my learning not only to code, but also with how to code with others. Through feedback, I was able to see some areas where I might be weak or strong in that I myself may have not noticed by myself. When others point out what I can do to improve for a future exercise, I see it as a learning opportunity to become better than what I was before.

From the feedback that I have recieved so far, I have tried to pseudocode more and think outloud more when coding with partners. I have also tried to make my code comments more concise and specific to make it easier for others to read.

Overall, I really enjoy pairing with others and recieving feedback. I find them very helpful to my learning experience because it allows me to see things in a different perspective and find areas of weakenesses or strengths that I may not be aware of myself.
